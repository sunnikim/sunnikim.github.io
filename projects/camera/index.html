What are the implications of the growing image culture in our society? We put so much weight on the value of sight, seeing is after all believing. The proliferation of digital images in our everyday lives alters the ways in which we perceive reality. Within these new frameworks of perception are power structures that serve to the cultural hegemony of Neo-liberalism. Beyond ideological impacts, we begin to value capturing the moment rather than living and experiencing it.

Too often one is satisfied with substituting real substance with superficial veneer of images, regardless of whether that is taking photographs in museums instead of attempting to connect with a painting or engineering a scene so that the photographed subjects may appear more aesthetically pleasing.

We put too much trust and value into cameras and digital images. What if they did not behave in a manner we want them to? What happens when..
